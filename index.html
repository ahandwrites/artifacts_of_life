Artifacts of Life by Amara Hand

AI Symposium 2026 • Research Remix: AI in the Real World
Built with Claude • Artifacts of Life • Amara Hand

Hi, I'm Amara Hand — a writing educator who was, not too long ago, genuinely scared of AI.

But eventually something shifted. Not because those fears disappeared — many of them are still quite valid — but because my frustrations and aspirations grew louder than my fear. I was tired of static, one-size-fits-all tools that didn't do justice to what my students actually needed. So I started building my own.

Here's what I know now: I was afraid because I didn't yet know everything I could do with it. When we engage these tools with good intention and imagination, AI doesn't replace our creativity — it makes space for us to flourish. The visions were always mine. AI just gave me the tools to build these out.

What I was doing has a name now: vibe-coding. Describing what I imagined, prompting my way toward that vision, and iterating until it worked. These are the artifacts that came out of it.
Interactive Apps & Tools
Quick Keys2026
A lot of my students were taking the long way to do really simple things: copying, pasting, selecting text. I built this keyboard simulator to teach them the shortcuts that make writing faster and less frustrating.

Lateral Reading Field Log2026
This started as a plain-text worksheet with a table, functional but static. I needed something students could actually use in the digital environment where they do most of their research, so I rebuilt it as an interactive tool for organizing sources and assessing credibility.
URL →Open ↗
How We Got Here: A Leadership Timeline2026
I made a static timeline mapping how the meaning of leadership has shifted across the century. This version is far more dynamic and interactive, and it finally does justice to Black American and global contexts that the original couldn't hold.
URL →Open ↗
Save the Grade Email Generator2026
This was built for TSI's Save the Grade event, a midterm intervention for students at risk of failing. It helps them write clear, concise emails that take accountability for where things went wrong and lay out a real plan for their comeback.
URL →Open ↗
The Student's AI Disclosure Form2025 / 2026
I wanted to promote transparency around AI use without making students start from scratch. This form gives them a structured way to reflect on how they used AI and disclose it to their professor, clearly and honestly.

Games
Locked in the Inbox: An Email-Writing Escape Room2026
I'd been wanting to build a digital escape room for a while but couldn't quite land on the concept. When I finally did, it made sense to center it on something my students and colleagues both struggle with: writing better emails.

Cite it or Not? A Common Knowledge Citation Activity2026
This one came to me early in my AI-curious days. I wanted a quick, low-stakes game that taught students something deceptively simple: what counts as common knowledge, and what actually needs a citation.

Custom GPT
Annotated Bibliography Building Assistant (ABBA)2025
When I was first introduced to PlayLab, I wanted to build something that walked students through a process they often rush: the annotated bibliography. The tool asks them questions about their source, so they have to prove they actually read it before the entry gets generated.

Other
Wedding Band Builder2026
For the grooms who say "I don't know" and the brides who love them anyway. This one was personal, a tool to help couples narrow down their wedding band preferences by walking through styles, metals, and what actually matters to them.

What Kind of User Are You?2026
A quick, Buzzfeed-style quiz to get students thinking about their own level of AI reliance. It's not meant to be heavy, just a fun nudge toward a little self-reflection.
